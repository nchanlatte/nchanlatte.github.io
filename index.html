Top Cites Visited

Hiroshima, Japan

In 2017 I spent 10 days with 3 people in Japan. In that time we visited Toykyo, Hiroshima and Kyoto, staying in hostels through hostelworld.com. We made our way around biking, taking the subway, and relaxing on Japan Rail. I would without a doubt return to Japan especially Hiroshima.

Rome, Italy

During my time at Creighton I had the opportinity to travel to 5 countries with my classmates. Three of the five was with Model United Nations (by far the best club out there). My senior year we went to Rome where we had an audience with the Pope, went to dinner with Foriegn Service Embassadors for the Vatican and enjoyed amazing italian cuisine.

Nagaland, India

While the picture above was a day trip to the the Taj Mahal, my favorite city in India was Nagaland. I spent a month in the northeast at a Jesuit school helping teach English and basic techonoligy to students. Being in Nagaland and with the Jesuits humbled me and taught me the power of knoweldge. Through that experience I am continusly reminded and inspired to continue to give back to the community, stay educated, and never take what I have for granted.

Barcelona, Spain

The food, culture, and architecture in Barcelona floored me. While I believe every city has its own beauty, I fell in love with Barcelona. The history of and design of all of Gaudi's buildings were impressive. The amount of time that it has taken to build and fund La Sagrada Familia church was unbeliveable. This colorful and vibrant city has to say the least left an unforgettable impact on me.

Seoul, South Korea

Fast internet, spotless subways and korean bbq. Each asian culture I've visited is so unqiue compared to anything I've experienced. Korea was such a friendly, clean and technologically advanced country. What draws my attention the most is how peaceful the country is with the constant threat of North Korea. The picture above is a train station the Koreans made for when the two countries declare peace. This station holds no trains but is a symbolic place for both sides. While the South Koreans are prepared for anything, they live their day with a smile on thier face.

New York, New York

My new home since 2017! I've always loved visiting NY and was excited to make the move out here. Everyday is a new adventure with challenages and rewards. I love the diversity, independance, constant change and tranquility of New York. I have yet to find a city quite like it.
